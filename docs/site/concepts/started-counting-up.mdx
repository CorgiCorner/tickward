---
title: "Started Counting Up"
description: "How one-off timers remain visible for review after they pass zero, and why repeating timers behave differently."
---

Started counting up is a review flow for **one-off timers**. It prevents a timer
from disappearing into Counting up before you have had a chance to notice that its
deadline passed. Repeating timers do not use this flow: when one occurrence
ends, their countdown advances directly to the next scheduled occurrence.

## What happens at zero

When a one-off timer reaches zero, its counter keeps running upward and the
timer appears in **Started counting up**, between Pinned and Upcoming. This is a
per-user review overlay, not a new timer status. The timer itself keeps its
normal countdown data and eventually belongs in Counting up.

An occurrence starts as **new**. After at least half of its card has remained
visible in the focused app for a short dwell, it becomes **waiting** for review.
Simply opening the project, receiving a notification, or viewing a section
header does not acknowledge it.

Pinned timers remain in Pinned with a **New** or **Counting up** badge, so the same
timer never appears twice. The project section shows only timers from the active
project. Project-switcher indicators show which other projects have new or
waiting occurrences without duplicating their timers in a global drawer.

## Acknowledge now or later

Choose **Acknowledge** when you have reviewed an occurrence. On the web app this
returns the timer to its natural place in Counting up, where it continues counting up.
Every manual acknowledgement, including bulk actions, offers Undo.

The compact **Acknowledge** menu can also schedule acknowledgement after 15
minutes, 1 hour, or 1 day, or leave the decision open until you acknowledge it.
A clock icon marks a scheduled acknowledgement without adding another line to
the card; hover or focus the icon to see the approximate remaining time. The
global policy in Settings can keep occurrences for review, move them directly
to Counting up, or apply a duration. Timed policies start only after the
occurrence is first shown to you; occurrences that have not yet been shown
never expire automatically.

These settings apply only to one-off timers. The **After zero** override is
therefore hidden when Repeat is enabled.

## Repeating timers

A repeating timer always counts down to its next effective occurrence. At an
occurrence boundary it may still fire its configured alarm or notification, but
it does not create a count-up review occurrence and never enters Started
counting up. The next render shows the time remaining until the following
occurrence.

For details about calendar slots and daylight saving behavior, see
[Recurrence](/concepts/recurrence). For the underlying clock calculation, see
[Countdown Accuracy](/concepts/countdown-accuracy).

## Persistence and offline crossings

For signed-in users, review state is per user and syncs across devices. One
member acknowledging a shared timer does not acknowledge it for anyone else.
Anonymous state remains in the browser for each local project.

When the app opens after being offline, naturally crossed one-off timers from
the previous 48 hours can be restored to review. Older elapsed timers and timers
created or manually edited directly into the past do not create a backlog.
